Yi-Shin Sheu, Ph.D.

I am a Cognitive Neuroscientist with 14+ years of experience in conducting brain-behavioral research. I am passionate about developing cognitive tasks and behavior metrics to gain insights of how humans perceive, use, and remember information. By gaining a basic understanding of how the brain works, it can provide useful insights on building technologies that are intuitive and human-centered. I am skilled at performing parametric modeling, multivariate pattern analysis, and clustering analysis to neuroimaging and behavioral data. I am passionate about applying my expertise to solve real world problems, particularly in the field of translational or clinical research, pharmaceutical or healthcare industry. My dream job will be one that would allow me to combine my passion of cognitive neuroscience and data science.

Research Summary

As we navigate the world around us, our brains are constantly bombarded with information. Working memory is the mental workspace where we can temporarily hold onto relevant information while performing metal operations. My research has centered around the neural mechanisms that prioritize and select information to be further processed in working memory. I use a wide range of methods including behavioral testing, neuroimaging, eye tracking, and brain stimulation to probe brain-behavior relationships. My recent research projects explore the predictive function of the cerebellum and its role in sequence learning.

Research Projects

How does the brain make predictions?

Cerebellum: predicting the future

The brain is constantly making predictions of the future states in what we see, what we do, and what we think. My research focused on the predictive function of the cerebellum in cognition. We tested the cerebellum's contribution to sequence prediction using TMS and fMRI.

How do we optimize task performance?

Tapping into our predictive minds

Sometimes a simple tweak in the task design can make tremendous difference in how efficiently we process stimuli. By having a feature that directly taps into the brain’s predictive power, it allows the participant to make predictions which greatly enhances task performance.
